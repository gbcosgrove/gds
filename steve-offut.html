Steve Offut

Vice President, Sustainability

Steve Offutt has more than twenty years’ of broad and deep experience in sustainability and environment. Mr. Offutt has significant ongoing experience negotiating, interacting and collaborating with stakeholders in government agencies, NGOs, and private sector companies, including FORTUNE 500 companies, on issues related to: climate change strategy, carbon markets, technology solutions, transportation, energy efficiency, environmental marketing & messaging, public-private partnerships and more.

He has led teams that have created, developed and implemented highly successful marketing and communication efforts, including the ENERGY STAR ® programs--arguably the most effective government environmental program in the history of the EPA. He represented the ENERGY STAR as its spokesperson at numerous conferences as well as for media coverage in print, on radio and television, even appearing on Bob Vila’s Home Again program. He is a LEED Accredited Professional, a green building certification administered by the US Green Building Council. He was the LEED AP for the Royal Netherlands Embassy's LEED Silver certification, achieved in November 2011, guiding the sustainability team through the LEED process from beginning to culmination.

Steve created, developed, implemented and led the innovative, EPA/business partnership—Best Workplaces for CommutersSM at EPA, providing significant value to the transportation-demand-management industry across the country. He grew this program faster than any previous EPA voluntary program and engaged more than 20% of the FORTUNE 500 companies to participate in the first two years. This program was considered so effective by the private sector that it was taken over by it, and it is still active within industry—years after it was defunded by the agency. Other work he did in the Office of Transportation and Air Quality included strategic planning for EPA’s SmartWay program, helping to guide the Office’s strategy toward voluntary climate programs and bringing the concept of market transformation to the office’s leadership.

Recently he has provided executive, strategic leadership to start-up companies, organizations and programs dealing in the sustainability space:

In the carbon markets industry, Mr. Offutt served as Chief Operating Officer for Carbonfund.org, a not-for-profit carbon offset provider and Business Development Director for The CarbonNeutral Company (formerly GreenLife), a start-up, private, carbon markets company.

Mr. Offutt was Vice President at NuRide, an innovative start-up that combines the power of Internet flexibility with ridesharing, in charge of all government relationships as well as several regional markets, including NuRide’s most profitable market, Houston.

In 2007, Arlington County, VA created its flagship sustainability initiative, Arlington Initiative to Reduce Emissions (AIRE), and brought Mr. Offutt on as Director of Transportation Projects and Strategic Advisor to assist with the initial start-up effort.

Mr. Offutt is Founder and Chair of the Green Business Roundtable – a DC-area network, since 2008, of individuals, organizations, institutions and companies working at the intersection of sustainability and business success.

Steve earned his MBA from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania focusing on corporate strategy, decisions sciences and the application of business concepts to city and regional planning as well as environmental issues. Prior to that he earned a B.Mus. from the University of Northern Colorado and a M.Mus from the University of Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music. He still occasionally performs both classical and jazz music and resides in Arlington, VA with his wife and two children.
